Other Important Information(Health Issues)

In 2003, Steve Jobs was diagnosed with a rare form of pancreatic cancer. In 2004, he underwent a reconstructive surgery known as the Whipple Operation, where parts of the pancreas, bile duct, intestine, and the gallbladder were removed. Jobs appeared to have recovered, until 2008, when he apparently lost weight, and adopted a sickly appearance. In January 2009, after questioning from the public, Jobs said that he was suffering from a hormonal imbalance, for which he would receive treatment and return shortly. However, he would then announce that he would be taking a personal leave through to the end of June. In June, it was reported that Jobs received a liver transplant, which led to speculation that the cancer had spread to his liver. Jobs would return to Apple at the end of June, as planned, and remained there until January of 2011, when he took another medical leave of absence. Jobs would resign as CEO in August, and he died two months later in October.
